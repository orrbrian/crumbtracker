Getting started

Open Settings and fill in the Body & activity section (weight, height, age, sex, activity). This unlocks BMI/BMR/TDEE and the Progress projection.

Pick a calorie target — type one in manually, or use the TDEE buttons (Use TDEE, TDEE − 500, TDEE + 300) to auto-fill based on your stats.

Diary

The Diary view shows one day at a time. Navigate with the arrows, "Today", or the date picker.

  • Totals bar — four cards showing Calories / Protein / Carbs / Fat vs your targets, with a progress bar each. Bars turn red when you go over.
  • Over-budget quip — a small italic line appears under the totals when you exceed calories. Light ribbing ≤10% over, sharper up to 25%, dry past 50%. Deterministic per (date, tier) so it doesn't flicker as you log.
  • Meal sections — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snacks. Click + Add food in a section header to jump to Foods with that section pre-selected. (Not to be confused with the Meals tab under Foods, which is for composed recipes — see that section below.)
  • Amount vs servings — when adding a food, the Amount input (g/ml) and Servings input are linked. Edit either; macros recalculate live. Last amount is remembered per food for next time.
  • Exercise — logged separately from meals. Calories burned never subtract from your "Calories in" total; they show up as Net calories in the right sidebar.
  • Notes — freeform text per day. Auto-saves 400 ms after you stop typing.
  • Right sidebar — shows the macro pie chart (protein/carbs/fat as % of calories) for the current diary date, plus the Net calories card when exercise is logged. On days with nothing logged, El Jefe greets you there instead.

Foods

  • Search — queries Open Food Facts. Typing a barcode (8/12/13/14 digits) also runs an exact lookup; that result gets an exact match badge and is pinned to the top.
  • 📷 Barcode scan — opens the webcam and reads UPC/EAN barcodes with ZXing. The live feed does continuous detection — aim and wait.
    • 📁 Image — decode a barcode from a saved photo or screenshot. Routes through the crop step.
    • 🖥 Desktop snip — see below. Works for both barcode and label scanners.
    • Type — manually enter the digits if the camera can't focus.
  • 📷 Label scan (OCR) — point the camera at a Nutrition Facts panel and click Capture, or upload an image. Tesseract.js reads the text locally; a parser pulls out calories / protein / carbs / fat / serving size. Values pre-fill a Custom Food form where you confirm name/brand and save. Accuracy is best on flat, well-lit, unfolded labels.
  • ✂️ Crop step — after Image, Capture, or Desktop snip, a green frame appears over the image. Drag the rectangle or its corners to cover just the barcode or nutrition panel, then click Scan region. Tight crops dramatically improve both barcode and OCR accuracy. Or click Use full image to skip cropping.
  • 🖥 Desktop snip — CrumbTracker hides, and a small always-on-top transparent window appears with a dashed green viewfinder and a draggable toolbar. Position and resize it over anything on your desktop (Amazon tab, PDF, another app's window, etc.), then Capture. The pixels under the frame feed into the crop step. Enter captures, Esc cancels.
  • Phone as a camera — any phone whose camera appears as a video device in the OS will show up in the camera dropdown:
    • macOS: iPhone's Continuity Camera (iOS 16+/macOS 13+) appears automatically.
    • Windows: install a virtual-webcam app (e.g. DroidCam, Iriun, or Camo) plus its phone app, then select it from the dropdown.
  • Custom foods — type nutrition values in directly. Attach a barcode so future scans resolve locally without hitting the API.
  • Food images — custom foods have an image picker at the top of the form (📁 Choose, 📋 Paste, or drop a file; Ctrl+V also pastes). Paste works with Windows Snipping Tool captures and with Copy Image from a browser (both bitmap and image URL clipboard payloads are handled). Search-result foods show an ✎ on their thumbnail inside the add-to-diary modal — click or drop to replace. Images are downscaled to 400 px JPEG data URLs and stored in the food record, so they travel with the food and work offline.
  • Nutrition missing? When a food has 0 kcal, the add-modal auto-expands a "Nutrition label" section. Enter values once — they're saved back to the food for next time.
  • (est.) tag — serving size was inferred from the product's category, not a real label. Double-check before logging.
  • Recent — foods you've logged recently, ready to re-add.

Meals

A meal is a saved recipe composed of foods you've already added — e.g. a sandwich built from bread, lettuce, turkey, and mayo. Use this when the same combination comes up repeatedly and you don't want to log each ingredient every time.

  • Create — open the Meals tab under Foods, click + New meal. Give it a name and (optionally) an image, then add ingredients one at a time: pick a food from the dropdown, enter how many servings of that food the meal uses, click + Add. Each ingredient row's servings can be edited inline; totals at the bottom update live.
  • Log to diary — click + Add on a saved meal. It goes in as a single diary entry (e.g. "Turkey sandwich · 1 serving · 410 kcal"), not as N separate ingredient rows. The entry snapshots totalled macros at the moment you log it.
  • Edit vs. history — editing a meal later (adjusting an ingredient's servings, swapping a food, etc.) never changes previously logged diary entries. Past meals are frozen at the macros they had when logged.
  • Upstream food changes — if you fix an ingredient food's nutrition (e.g. correct the bread's calorie count), future meal additions will use the updated value automatically, because meals reference ingredients by ID.
  • Missing ingredients — if you delete a food that's still used by a meal, that meal shows a ⚠ N missing note. It will still log, but totals will exclude the missing items until you edit the meal and remove or replace them.
  • Servings vs. amount — meal ingredients are counted in the ingredient food's own servings, not grams. If a food's serving size is 30g and you use half a serving in your meal, enter 0.5.

Progress

  • Plan line (grey dashed) — projected weight assuming you hit your calorie target every day. Slope is (TDEE + planned exercise − target) / 7700 kcal/kg.
  • Weigh-ins (green dots, connected when ≥ 2) — your actual data. Click + Log weight to record one. The plan line re-anchors at your latest weigh-in, and your body profile's current weight auto-updates to the most recent entry.
  • Planned exercise (kcal/day) — extra calories your plan assumes you burn beyond TDEE. Bumps the deficit; steepens the plan line. Saved between sessions.
  • Range — 1w / 2w / 1m / 3m / 6m / 1y. Window is centered on today.
  • Stats row — Current weight, Plan rate (kg or lb per week), projected weight at end of range, and Net change. Hover each card for the formula behind it.
  • Weigh-in list below the chart, newest first; each row has a delete button.

Settings

  • Daily targets — calorie and macro goals (grams). Totals bar progress tracks against these.
  • Body & activity — drives BMR, TDEE, and BMI. Auto-saves as you type.
  • Units — toggle lb/ft·in ↔ kg/cm at any time. Existing values convert automatically.
  • Reset — wipes every object store (foods, entries, settings, notes, weigh-ins, exercise). Double-confirmed. Useful before sharing the app.

Formulas

  • BMI = weight(kg) / height(m)². Categories: <18.5 Underweight · 18.5–24.9 Normal · 25–29.9 Overweight · 30+ High.
  • BMR — Mifflin–St Jeor.
    Male: 10·kg + 6.25·cm − 5·age + 5
    Female: 10·kg + 6.25·cm − 5·age − 161
  • TDEE = BMR × activity multiplier (Sedentary 1.2 · Light 1.375 · Moderate 1.55 · Active 1.725 · Very active 1.9).
  • Weight change — 7700 kcal ≈ 1 kg (3500 kcal ≈ 1 lb). A rough rule; real humans vary.
  • Macro calories — Protein 4 kcal/g · Carbs 4 kcal/g · Fat 9 kcal/g. Used for the sidebar pie chart.

Data & privacy

Everything is stored locally in IndexedDB inside this app's user-data directory. Object stores: foods, entries, settings, notes, weights, exercise, meals.

The app only talks to the network for Open Food Facts (world.openfoodfacts.org, search.openfoodfacts.org) when you search or scan a barcode. Nothing you log leaves your machine.

Shortcuts & tips

  • Enter in the search box runs the search. If the query is a valid-length all-digit string (8/12/13/14), an exact barcode lookup also runs in parallel.
  • In the add-food modal, typing a new Amount recomputes Servings automatically. Works the other way too.
  • Scanning the same barcode twice hits the local cache on the second scan — instant, no network.
  • Desktop snip viewfinder: Enter captures, Esc cancels.
  • Hover most numbers and buttons for a tooltip explaining what they mean.
  • Open DevTools with Ctrl+Shift+I if something looks off.
  • Ctrl+R reloads the renderer. Changes to Electron's main process (e.g. IPC handlers) require a full app restart instead.
