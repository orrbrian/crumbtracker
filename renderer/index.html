Getting started

Open Settings and fill in the Body & activity section (weight, height, age, sex, activity). This unlocks BMI/BMR/TDEE and the Progress projection.

Pick a calorie target — type one in manually, or use the TDEE buttons (Use TDEE, TDEE − 500, TDEE + 300) to auto-fill based on your stats.

Diary

The Diary view shows one day at a time. Navigate with the arrows, "Today", or the date picker.

  • Totals bar — four cards showing Calories / Protein / Carbs / Fat vs your targets, with a progress bar each. Bars turn red when you go over.
  • Over-budget quip — a small italic line appears under the totals when you exceed calories. Light ribbing ≤10% over, sharper up to 25%, dry past 50%. Deterministic per (date, tier) so it doesn't flicker as you log.
  • Meal sections - Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snacks. Click + Add food in a section header to jump to Foods with that section pre-selected. (Not to be confused with the Meals tab under Foods, which is for composed recipes - see that section below.)
  • Edit entries in place - each logged entry has a ✎ pencil next to the ✕. Click it to reopen the Add dialog pre-filled with the entry's amount, servings, meal section, and date. Saving replaces the original entry with your edits (no double-count).
  • ↻ Copy from... - each meal section has a copy button that pulls that section's entries from another day into today. Quick shortcuts for Yesterday / 2 days ago / Last week, or pick any date. A preview shows what'll be copied before you commit.
  • 📄 Export PDF - button next to the date navigation. Exports 1 to 7 days of diary as a single PDF (Letter, portrait). Shortcuts for Today only / Last 3 days / Last 7 days, or pick a custom range. Empty days in a multi-day export are skipped. The PDF is a clean light-theme layout with totals per day, meal tables (name, amount, kcal, P/C/F), exercise, and notes. Uses Chromium's built-in PDF engine - text stays selectable and searchable, images are vector where possible.
  • Amount vs servings — when adding a food, the Amount input (g/ml) and Servings input are linked. Edit either; macros recalculate live. Last amount is remembered per food for next time.
  • Exercise — logged separately from meals. Calories burned never subtract from your "Calories in" total; they show up as Net calories in the right sidebar.
  • Notes — freeform text per day. Auto-saves 400 ms after you stop typing.
  • Right sidebar - shows a concentric-ring macro chart for the current diary date. The outer ring is today's actual macro split by calories; the inner ring is your goal split (from Settings). Matching splits align; drift shows as misaligned arc ends. Hover any slice for exact grams and percentage. The Net calories card appears when exercise is logged. On days with no food or targets, El Jefe greets you there instead.

Foods

  • Search — queries Open Food Facts. Typing a barcode (8/12/13/14 digits) also runs an exact lookup; that result gets an exact match badge and is pinned to the top.
  • 📷 Barcode scan — opens the webcam and reads UPC/EAN barcodes with ZXing. The live feed does continuous detection — aim and wait.
    • 📁 Image — decode a barcode from a saved photo or screenshot. Routes through the crop step.
    • 🖥 Desktop snip — see below. Works for both barcode and label scanners.
    • Type — manually enter the digits if the camera can't focus.
  • 📷 Label scan (OCR) — point the camera at a Nutrition Facts panel and click Capture, or upload an image. Tesseract.js reads the text locally; a parser pulls out calories / protein / carbs / fat / serving size. Values pre-fill a Custom Food form where you confirm name/brand and save. Accuracy is best on flat, well-lit, unfolded labels.
  • ✂️ Crop step — after Image, Capture, or Desktop snip, a green frame appears over the image. Drag the rectangle or its corners to cover just the barcode or nutrition panel, then click Scan region. Tight crops dramatically improve both barcode and OCR accuracy. Or click Use full image to skip cropping.
  • 🖥 Desktop snip — CrumbTracker hides, and a small always-on-top transparent window appears with a dashed green viewfinder and a draggable toolbar. Position and resize it over anything on your desktop (Amazon tab, PDF, another app's window, etc.), then Capture. The pixels under the frame feed into the crop step. Enter captures, Esc cancels.
  • Phone as a camera — any phone whose camera appears as a video device in the OS will show up in the camera dropdown:
    • macOS: iPhone's Continuity Camera (iOS 16+/macOS 13+) appears automatically.
    • Windows: install a virtual-webcam app (e.g. DroidCam, Iriun, or Camo) plus its phone app, then select it from the dropdown.
  • Custom foods - type nutrition values in directly. Attach a barcode so future scans resolve locally without hitting the API. The My Foods tab has a filter box at the top - start typing to narrow by name or brand.
  • Food images - custom foods have an image picker at the top of the form (📁 Choose, 📋 Paste, or drop a file; Ctrl+V also pastes). The Add to diary dialog also lets you edit the food's image inline: click the thumbnail (or the ✎ pencil) for a file picker, click the 📋 button to paste from clipboard, drop a file onto the thumb, or Ctrl+V anywhere in the dialog. Paste works with Windows Snipping Tool captures and with Copy Image from a browser (both bitmap and image-URL clipboard payloads are handled). Images are downscaled to 400 px JPEG data URLs and stored on the food record, so they travel with the food and work offline.
  • Nutrition missing? When a food has 0 kcal, the add-modal auto-expands a "Nutrition label" section. Enter values once — they're saved back to the food for next time.
  • (est.) tag — serving size was inferred from the product's category, not a real label. Double-check before logging.
  • Recent - foods you've logged recently, ready to re-add.
  • Save to My Foods - a checkbox on the Add-food dialog. When you're adding a food from Search or Recent, tick it to also copy the food into your My Foods tab for easy re-adding later. Foods already in My Foods show "Already in My Foods" instead.

Meals

A meal is a saved recipe composed of foods you've already added — e.g. a sandwich built from bread, lettuce, turkey, and mayo. Use this when the same combination comes up repeatedly and you don't want to log each ingredient every time.

  • Create — open the Meals tab under Foods, click + New meal. Give it a name and (optionally) an image, then add ingredients one at a time: pick a food from the dropdown, enter how many servings of that food the meal uses, click + Add. Each ingredient row's servings can be edited inline; totals at the bottom update live.
  • Log to diary — click + Add on a saved meal. It goes in as a single diary entry (e.g. "Turkey sandwich · 1 serving · 410 kcal"), not as N separate ingredient rows. The entry snapshots totalled macros at the moment you log it.
  • Edit vs. history — editing a meal later (adjusting an ingredient's servings, swapping a food, etc.) never changes previously logged diary entries. Past meals are frozen at the macros they had when logged.
  • Upstream food changes — if you fix an ingredient food's nutrition (e.g. correct the bread's calorie count), future meal additions will use the updated value automatically, because meals reference ingredients by ID.
  • Missing ingredients — if you delete a food that's still used by a meal, that meal shows a ⚠ N missing note. It will still log, but totals will exclude the missing items until you edit the meal and remove or replace them.
  • Servings vs. amount — meal ingredients are counted in the ingredient food's own servings, not grams. If a food's serving size is 30g and you use half a serving in your meal, enter 0.5.

Phone scan experimental

Let a phone on the same Wi-Fi act as a barcode scanner for CrumbTracker. Useful when the product isn't near your PC - flip the box downstairs, scan it with your phone, the add-to-diary dialog pops up on the desktop.

How it works: clicking 📱 Phone scan in the Foods view starts a small HTTPS server on your local network and shows a QR code. Your phone scans the QR to open a scanner page. Barcodes the phone reads are sent back to CrumbTracker over the LAN. Nothing leaves your network - there's no cloud, no account, no external server.

Phone side - step by step

  1. Same Wi-Fi. The phone and the PC must be on the same network. A phone on cellular data will not work.
  2. Scan the QR code with your phone's camera app (iOS Camera / Google Lens / any QR app). Tap the link it offers.
  3. You will see a security warning. Something like "Your connection is not private" or "Not secure". This is expected - CrumbTracker uses a self-signed certificate for the LAN session. No certificate authority is involved because the server only exists on your home network.
    • Chrome / Edge on Android: tap Advanced then Proceed to (IP) (unsafe).
    • Firefox on Android: tap Advanced, then Accept the risk and continue.
    • Safari on iOS: tap Show Details, then visit this website, then Visit Website and enter your passcode / Face ID if prompted.
  4. Allow camera access when the phone's browser asks. CrumbTracker uses the camera locally - the video feed itself never leaves your phone; only the decoded barcode digits are sent.
  5. Point at a UPC / EAN barcode. Hold the phone roughly parallel to the label, 4-8 inches away, let the camera focus. Fill most of the reticle with the bars. Flat, front-lit, unwrinkled labels decode fastest.
  6. When the phone says ✓ Sent, the desktop opens the add-food dialog. Scan the next product immediately - no need to re-pair.

Desktop side

  • The 📱 Phone scan button shows a green pulsing dot while a session is active. Click it while active to re-open the QR (useful if your phone's tab closed) or to Stop sharing.
  • The session auto-stops after 10 minutes of no activity, or when you click Stop sharing.
  • If you see multiple URLs in the modal, your PC has several network interfaces. The first one is usually the right one; if the phone can't connect, try typing one of the other URLs into the phone's browser manually.

Troubleshooting

  • "TLS error" / "Can't provide a secure connection" - usually means the phone landed on the wrong IP, or the PC's firewall is blocking the port. Check that the URL in the modal matches your home Wi-Fi's subnet (usually starts with 192.168.).
  • Scanning is slow or nothing happens - the phone's autofocus needs a moment. Hold steady, closer to the barcode, with good lighting.
  • QR code doesn't open a browser - some phone camera apps treat https:// URLs oddly. Type the URL shown under the QR into your phone's browser manually.
  • First scan after a long idle - the session may have timed out. Click 📱 Phone scan on the desktop again to start a fresh session.

Privacy & security

  • The server only runs while you have an active session. A random 128-bit token must match in the URL, so nothing on your LAN can guess the endpoint.
  • A fresh self-signed TLS certificate is generated per session. No cert is ever installed on your phone or PC.
  • Don't use Phone scan on public Wi-Fi. It's designed for your home network only.

Progress

  • Plan line (grey dashed) — projected weight assuming you hit your calorie target every day. Slope is (TDEE + planned exercise − target) / 7700 kcal/kg.
  • Weigh-ins (green dots, connected when ≥ 2) — your actual data. Click + Log weight to record one. The plan line re-anchors at your latest weigh-in, and your body profile's current weight auto-updates to the most recent entry.
  • Planned exercise (kcal/day) — extra calories your plan assumes you burn beyond TDEE. Bumps the deficit; steepens the plan line. Saved between sessions.
  • Range — 1w / 2w / 1m / 3m / 6m / 1y. Window is centered on today.
  • Stats row — Current weight, Plan rate (kg or lb per week), projected weight at end of range, and Net change. Hover each card for the formula behind it.
  • Weigh-in list below the chart, newest first; each row has a delete button.

Settings

The Settings view flows top-to-bottom in the order you'd typically fill it out: enter your body stats first to get TDEE, then set daily targets (using the TDEE buttons or a macro preset).

  • Body & activity - drives BMR, TDEE, and BMI. Auto-saves as you type. Once filled in, the Use TDEE / TDEE - 500 / TDEE + 300 buttons appear under it; clicking one writes that value into the Daily calories field below.
  • Units - toggle lb/ft·in vs kg/cm at any time. Existing values convert automatically.
  • Daily targets - calorie and macro goals (grams). Totals bar and macro rings on the diary track against these.
  • Macro preset - a dropdown with conventional splits (Balanced, Weight loss, Bulking, Body recomp, Low carb, Keto, Endurance, High protein). Pick one to auto-fill Protein/Carbs/Fat grams from your current Daily calories. Editing any macro manually flips it back to Custom. Hover each option for what it's typically used for. These are industry-convention splits - not medical advice.
  • Backup & restore - Export data... downloads a JSON file containing every store (foods, entries, meals, settings, notes, weights, exercise). Keep the file somewhere safe as a backup, or use it to move your data to another PC. Import data... replaces all current data with the contents of a previously exported file - prompt-confirmed, and irreversible once accepted, so export current data first if you want a fallback.
  • Reset - wipes every object store (foods, entries, settings, notes, weigh-ins, exercise, meals). Double-confirmed. Useful before sharing the app or starting fresh.

Formulas

  • BMI = weight(kg) / height(m)². Categories: <18.5 Underweight · 18.5–24.9 Normal · 25–29.9 Overweight · 30+ High.
  • BMR — Mifflin–St Jeor.
    Male: 10·kg + 6.25·cm − 5·age + 5
    Female: 10·kg + 6.25·cm − 5·age − 161
  • TDEE = BMR × activity multiplier (Sedentary 1.2 · Light 1.375 · Moderate 1.55 · Active 1.725 · Very active 1.9).
  • Weight change — 7700 kcal ≈ 1 kg (3500 kcal ≈ 1 lb). A rough rule; real humans vary.
  • Macro calories — Protein 4 kcal/g · Carbs 4 kcal/g · Fat 9 kcal/g. Used for the sidebar pie chart.

Data & privacy

Everything is stored locally in IndexedDB inside this app's user-data directory. Object stores: foods, entries, settings, notes, weights, exercise, meals.

The app only talks to the network for Open Food Facts (world.openfoodfacts.org, search.openfoodfacts.org) when you search or scan a barcode. Nothing you log leaves your machine.

Phone scan (experimental) runs a temporary HTTPS server on your LAN only while the session is active. It's bound to your PC's network interfaces, authenticated by a random token, and auto-stops on idle. No external server or account is involved. See the Phone scan section for details.

Shortcuts & tips

  • Enter in the search box runs the search. If the query is a valid-length all-digit string (8/12/13/14), an exact barcode lookup also runs in parallel.
  • In the add-food modal, typing a new Amount recomputes Servings automatically. Works the other way too.
  • Scanning the same barcode twice hits the local cache on the second scan - instant, no network.
  • Desktop snip viewfinder: Enter captures, Esc cancels.
  • Hover most numbers and buttons for a tooltip explaining what they mean.
  • Open DevTools with Ctrl+Shift+I (or F12) if something looks off.
  • Ctrl+R reloads the renderer. Changes to Electron's main process (e.g. IPC handlers) require a full app restart instead.
  • Single-instance: launching CrumbTracker a second time focuses the existing window instead of starting a duplicate. IndexedDB only allows one writer per database, so duplicate instances would fail to load anyway.
